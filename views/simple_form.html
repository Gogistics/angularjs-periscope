On this page we have one controller, hence one |$scope|, and one form.
  1. Click the Peri$scope #rb# (not the form #rb#) to delete the images and clear the diff history. Check the "key" checkbox and then click the #psb# to see the initial state. |formCtrl| is an instance of FormController and |modelCtrl| is an instance of NgModelController. Peri$scope shows select properties of those controllers.
    TBD: Someday, Peri$scope might be enhanced to show FormControllers and NgModelControllers as separate nodes in the graph/picture.
  2. Uncheck the "key" checkbox. Type something into the textbox, click the #psb#. Property |prop1| is updated, and various properties of the FormController and NgModelController are updated.
  3. Click the form #rb#, click the #psb#. Notice the updates to the FormController and NgModelController properties.
  4. Type something into the textbox, click the submit button, click the #psb#. Of the many properies that changed, notice that |$submitted| is now |true|.
  5. TBD: add validation, to show how properties |$valid|, |$invalid| and |$error| change.

Best Practice: |ng-model| should be used with model properties that the |$scope| references. I.e., have a "dot" in your ng-model expression. Listen to Misko if that didn't make sense.
